Re: [samuraihistory] Japanese castles

by herbertlison

Unfortunately the current Osaka castle is a modern reconstruction and only
a pale imitation of the one built by Hideyoshi in the late 1500s. Although
many historical structures were destroyed in WWII, there are still
a number of castles that are more or less "original" construction,
with a limited amount of restoration work over the years.
The most famous to westerners is Himeji, sometimes called
the "White Heron" castle. However there are many others, including
my personal favorite, Inuyama, a tiny jewel of a castle on the Kiso River,
north of Nagoya.

Re: [samuraihistory] Japanese castles

by William letham

Well that would take some volumes to describe in detail the two sieges of
Osaka jo. Basically the winter campaign ended in a stalemate. During the
truce Ieyasu managed to have the Toyotomi side fill in the moats this
allowed him more access during the summer campaign. The Toyotomi side
realizing this lead its troops in a massive land battle outside the castle
but were defeated.

Perhaps the most detailed account in English would be in James Murdochs
second volume of his history of Japan.
