by rodsloan

Hi and welcome to the group.

You have many questions, and I'd suggest looking through messages on this group and checking out Wikipedia to get a basic idea of things.

1: Samurai armor is basically made up of many parts that are tied together. There are many different styles and types of armor, but have a look at images on the web and you'll get some idea.

2: A Samurai's swords were like a badge of their rank, this changed over time. Also, the Samurai believed their swords had a soul. But, if you think about it, when everyone else has a sword to kill you with, wouldn't you value your own swords more?

3: The katana and wakizashi.

4: For killing people and making you look tough so that other people wouldn't try to kill you. The katana, which was the longest of the two, was your main weapon, while the wakizashi was your backup and the easiest sword to use in doors. You could use both swords together or just the best for the job at hand. Note that at some times the samurai might only have his wakizashi, such as when in court.

7: Bushido means "way of the warrior" or "warrior way" and was a set of principles Samurai were supposed to live by, very much like the European knights code of chivalry.

1. Armor :
Depending on your status and time period, this changes---as everyone else has said.

2. The sword was not the samurai's most prized possession. My family, which is samurai descendants from 2 sides, values the lifes of the family members, the Buddhist Dharma, and the family knowledge alot more than some sharpened piece of metal.
The 'sword is the samurai soul' was an attempt to neuter the samurai during the Edo period. Marx called it "aienation of the species being"... does that make sense?
The samurai's soul is the Clan... not some over-sized knife.

3.) Why so much care about swords? You do understand that some samurai used spears right? Some used guns ... swords are so over-played these days. In my own experience, the cord of your sword seems to have more uses than the blade itself !

4.) What are all swords used for ?

5.) Bushido means, when you get to the heart, giving your life for the person whom you've sworn your service to. It means proving the principle of the warrior beyond your own selfish fears.
But... in reality--- "bushido" is really a system the Tokugawa Shogunate created to keep a country of jittery samurai in check. Let's be honest.. it was really a system of social control.
So, you have TWO bushido.. the actual historical bushido, which was a social control device... and the "philosophical bushido"... which is an every-changing definition of what it means to be a warrior.

85% of what you hear about samurai is Edo Period castration doctrine OR Post-Meiji bullshito.. either neutering the samurai or whoring the samurai out to the commoners..

I mean.. how DARE the northern imposter dare use Kusunoki Masashige during WW2 ? Telling all the commoners to associate themselves with a Southern Court hero ? Because the northern pretenders lack virtue, the outcome of WW2 was inevitable ..
... there is some real bushido for you.

Now, because of this pretender.. there are no samurai anymore.. the southern court does not sit on the throne..

.. the country is doomed.
