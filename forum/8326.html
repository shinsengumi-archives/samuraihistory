Totok,

While NHK's saigo dramas are well known for accuracy, Shinsengumi was a
departure from their usual high standards. In an attempt to widen the viewer
base, NHK used pop stars instead of traditional actors in at least one of
the roles and "modified" accepted history. This did have the desired effect
of increasing viewers, but there was an uproar among japanese historians
over the actual content.

M.J.Peters

Re: taiga drama - shinsengumi

by shikisokuzekukusokuzeshiki8

I have DVD box set of the series.
This series was ranked 2nd of the most popular Taiga drama.
http://ranking.goo.ne.jp/ranking.php?C1=022&C2=nhk_taigadrama

The script writer Mitani Kouki is comedy writer, so the drama is
pretty funny.
The historical accuracy is 85%(my estimate)
He added some fictional episodes like
-Kondou and Hijikata and Sakamoto Ryouma and Katsura Kogoro were
friends
-Saitou Hajime saved by Kondou in Edo
-Kondou's wife to confront Kondou's mistress
etc

Episode of Yamanami's seppuku was really really sad.
NHK received hundreds of "save Yamanami petitions" from viewers.
Viewers also requested NHK to make following episode for Hijikata
which was aired in January 3rd.

Shinsengumi!! The last day of Hijikata Toshizou
http://www.shinsengumi-exp.jp/
