Hi Gustav

I stand to be corrected of course... but although the design appears to be a "MON" (Family Crest), of three crescent shaped birds intertwined at 120degrees, I feel that it is a modern creation and not "traditional". It is a lot easier to say what it is not rather than what it is.... There are a few native birds represented in various mon - Crane, Sparrow, Wild Goose, Crow, Mandarin Duck, Dove, Hawk and the domestic Chicken (I suppose we should also add the Pheonix!) - non of these are similar to the ones represented in the "Ghost Dog" crest. The best description of which I would say Swan or Goose. Having said all this I know I have in my collection a crest design of a bird in flight similar in nature to the birds in your crest... I believe it came from a modern japanese design book but cannot as yet remember!!

Best wishes with the investigations...

Rod Johnson
