by ltdomer98

Rich-

YAY! SUCCESS!

Yes, that's a jingasa. As far as stylistic dating
goes, I'm not entirely sure. Most of the sengoku era
jingasa I've seen in illustrations are a little
flatter, and are made of iron, as you would expect of
military headgear. I believe during this time you had
two types: iron jingasa the Ashigaru used as headgear,
and the straw/thatch versions used by the peasantry,
monks, women, etc. During the Edo period is when I
believe you saw a switch to your average samurai
wearing them as headgear, and you see the switch to
lacquered versions.

Of course, I really have no idea. Still looking
though.

Nate
