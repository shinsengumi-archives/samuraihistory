The Gatling gun was apparently first used at Petersburg, VA, in the American
Civil War in 1864-5. It was formally adopted in 1866 by the US Army. Indeed,
one of the factors that brought about Custer's disastrous defeat in 1876 was
that he apparently disdained to bring along a Gatling gun detachment because
it would slow him up! Thus, it is entirely possible that a friendly country
like Meiji Japan could have been shipped some by the 1877 rebellion. Prof Murf
