Useless trivia:

It appears that his real name was not in fact Saigo Takamori.
Takamori was his father's name; his name was Takanaga.

There are at least 2 versions of the story as to how this came about.
My preferred version of the story goes that he was going to obtain
some rank and was to be sent a certificate by the emperor. He was
unavailable and a friend of his was asked to confirm the name that
should be used on the certificate. The friend misremembered the name
and gave the father's name instead: "Saigo Takamori". This was
probably due to the fact that given names were hardly used. If a
"name" was used at all (instead of a title) it was probably a
nickname. Saigo's nickname was "Kichi-no-suke".

When Saigo got the (incorrect) certificate, he apparently just went
with the flow and from then on used "Takamori" instead of his given
name, Takanaga.
