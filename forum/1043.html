Re: [samuraihistory] Samurai Movies

by Tom Helm

DAIBOSATSU TOGE

Is a Taisho period novelization of the Meiji Ishhin and its aftermath, It ran
for several years and was made into many movies. Most notably THE SWORD OF DOOM
which many are unaware was supposed to be the first of a trilogy. The older pre
war version features some Satsuma rebellion scenes but I know of no others.
Would love to see it all realized on the silver screen myself - Tom

SWORD OF DOOM is my very favorite film BTW...

Re: [samuraihistory] Samurai Movies

by Mikiko Letham

There are two miniseries about the life of Saigo Takamori that includes the
Satsuma rebellion.

Takaharazaka starring Satomi Kotaro originally aired in 1987. Its about 5hrs
long.
Tobu ga Gotoku starring Nishida Toshiyuki was 1990s Taiga drama offering, a
fifty part maxiseries.

Both of these are available on video but I assume only here in Japan and
only in Japanese. the Tobu ga Gotoku was shortened to about 7 hours for the
videotape.
