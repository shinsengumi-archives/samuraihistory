The June updates are mostly done at the Electronic Journals of Martial
Arts and Sciences http://ejmas.com/ and you can check out the new articles
at http://ejmas.com/thismonth.html

Some pretty good items this month once again, I'm always amazed at what
our editors and writers (who are all volunteers) manage to pull together
for you.

When you click through to the site you may encounter a bit of sluggishness
once in a while. This is because there's been a major denial of service
attack going on against a few big ISPs and our host servers are included
in the fun. They've upgraded and the site is running but you may hit the
occasional hiccup. Just reload your page and be a little patient and
you'll get where you're going. We designed the site for high transfer
rates and low "bells and whistles" so it seems to be OK so far.

Any graphic designers out there want to work on a small project each
month? If so drop me a line, I've got an idea that may be some fun.

Don't forget to check out the EJMAS site itself, lots of archived material
to dig through with 8 publications and an encyclopedia. If you're
researching in the martial arts I highly recommend checking us out first.

And don't forget please that all of this is free for your use. Do help us
keep it up by perhaps clicking on the tips jar while reading an especially
good article, or visiting one of our sponsors who help us pay the
bills. You may be able to buy your next piece of equipment cheaper down at
the dollar store but those guys aren't spending several hours every month
making sure you've got your regular fix of entertainment and education now
are they?

There are seminars in July on both the east and west coasts of North
America sponsored by EJMAS so check them out from the main page
http://ejmas.com/ You'll find some good instruction there too.

Once more, I'd like to invite everyone to copy this email to any person or
list you think would welcome it.

Kim Taylor
