Keiman-
I am sure you mean Kochi on Shikoku. Home of Sakamoto Ryoma who lived
in Kochi when it was the captiol of Tosa-han. He was a loyalist, who
understood the threat presented by the Western powers as well as the
opportunities for trade and growth that might ensue from intercourse
with those same nations.
While the Bakufu struggled to restore its former glory and the
Southwestern han struggled to bring it down, Ryoma sought a "Third
Way". Sadly he was killed before his genius could be fully utilized in
the service of his country.
He was indeed an accomplished swordsman and is in fact MORE respected
because he never killed another man in anger, or to further his cause...
