Re: [samuraihistory] Question...

by ltdomer98

Yes.

Firearms were an aid, not the deciding factor. The
terrain was not conducive to a Takeda charge, the
combination of the Rengo-gawa river, the mud, and the
Oda positions in the foothills, combined with the
palisade, made for a great disruptor. The effect with
the firearms was definitely a huge factor, but
considering the Oda had the terrain advantage in
ADDITION to outnumbering the Takeda 2 to 1, I firmly
believe that they would have won. Would it have been
as lopsided? No, it would have been much closer. As it
was, the fighting was very fierce, and the Oda had
over 6,000 casualties as it was.

Royal armouries UK

by umaryu

Hi all

I was at the royal armouries Leeds UK yesterday. as
always i took loads of photos.

The sword cabinet has changed since my last visit and
now contains part of a provate collection. This
included 2 swords by Gassan Sadakazu and Gassan
Sadakatsu. The Sadakatsu was made to commemorate the
birth of emperor Akihito.

My favourite was the Nagamaki Naoshi which was
converted to a katana. Made in around 1300, it had a
dotanuki style kissaki.

Many of you have privately had photos from me before,
but to save recieving and sending laods of emails I
have put them all into zipped files on my web site for
you to download.

http://www.hanako.co.uk/Armouries/Armouries.htm

Have fun with them

paul
