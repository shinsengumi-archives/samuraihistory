There was a sort of bio on Yoritomo by Jeffery P Mass called "Yoritomo and
the founding of the first Bakufu".

Conrad Totman also wrote a bio on Tokugawa Ieyasu.

"Kenmu: Go-Daigos Revolution" by Andrew Goble is about Go-Daigo Tenno (not
strictly bio though)

"Two Japanese Christian Heroes" by Johannes Laures SJ covers Takayama Ukon
and Hosokawa Gracia

A rather odd book I did come across was "Reflections on the Life and Times
of Motonari Mori" by Dr. Shimizu Usui. He is a medical doctor and the book
is mostly about the medical conditions of the sengoku bushi.

I heard that Jeffrey Kurashige is working on a bio of Takeda Shingen.

There is a very old article on Date Masamune in TASJ from the 1890s (vol 21
pp 1-105.)

By the way I am reading Keene's book on Yoshimasa right now. I wish someone
would write a bio on Nobunaga the size and scope of his work on Meiji tenno.
