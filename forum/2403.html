Considering the question posed about sword metals, to my knowledge, all or
most of the extant samurai blades are of steel, or possibly the most early
Japanese swords of iron. In the Middle East, bronze was used as a metal for
swords in the early Dynasties of Egypt. But it was the introduction of iron as
the cutting edge---sorry about that !!!!!!-----in warfare technology that
allowed what could be called the Military Revolution of ancient times, the
bringing of Rome and Greece to prominence in the Mediterranean region. Brass, to
the best of my knowledge, was never a metal used in the making of swords,
but has always been used in the makings of fittings for the swords and their
scabbards. All the best, Professor Murphy, The Japan Foundation
