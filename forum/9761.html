Fourth Annual Samurai Fiction Contest, 2008

The parameters for the third annual Samurai Fiction contest are covered on
this page. The stories must involve Japan or the Samurai, and the story may
take place any time up to, but no later than the end of the 19th century, and
may take place at any point in Japanese history before this.

Requirements:

This is to be a piece of fiction writing taking place within an unspecified
period in Japanese history up to the late 19th century. The available genres
are listed on the website, linked above - the stories must be written in the
style of one of the genres on the listed.

Parameters:

The story is required to be more than 1,000 words, with a 5,000 word
targeted limit - overages are acceptable if necessary, but please don't overdo it.
It can be done in the first person, or the third person, and must be a
complete short story, not an excerpt from a larger story. The genre(s) must be
listed at the top of the story above the title - one genre or a combination of two
genres are acceptable. Although the particular genre will not affect
judging, all entrants must make sure that their story follows the requirements of
their chosen genre(s) - see details on the website listed above.

Sequels: Sequels to previous stories entered in the contest are acceptable -
however, the story must be a stand-alone short story, not a continuation of
the previous story.
