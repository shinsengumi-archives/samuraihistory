Knutsen's + Kure's books

by deanwayland

Hi Folks,

I've been somewhat "off-planet" for several months, good to be back in
the land of samurai-history again; anyway...

>While looking around for Knutsen's 'Japanese Spears:
>Polearms and Their Use in Old Japan' that was highly
>recomend by a few on this list, I came across another of
>his books, 'Japanese Polearms'. Does any one have this?
>Is it highly recomended?
>The only problem is that most places don't have it in stock
>(it's from 1963), the one place I did find it is selling it
>for US$395.00.

Fortunately I'm lucky enough to have both of these books, and to have
bought them when they were way more affordable. The second is in essence
an update of the first, with some differences in content, but not 400
dollars worth. I would recommend getting his latest book and borrowing
the other through inter-library loan if you can, so you can compare the
two without crashing your bank account.

>Also does anyone have Mitsuo Kure's 'SAMURAI: An
>Illustrated History'? What's your impression of this one?

Again a nice book, and worth having for the photographs. Unlike his
previous work, this is laid out in chronological order, so you can see
the development of the kit. The book also has sketches by Dr Sasama, aka
Mr Japanese armour, who sadly died last year.

Hope this is of some help.

Yours

Dean

Gentlemen
The book JAPANESE POLEARMS by Knutsen was quite innovative at its time of
