> in my research of yoshitsune and the minamoto as a whole - i have so

Yoshitsune was the ninth son.

Eldest was Yoshihira, then Tomonaga, then Yoritomo, Yoshidako, Mareyoshi,
Noriyori, Zenjou, Gien, and Yoshitsune. There were also two daughters.

Yoshihira (1140-60). Executed after the Heiji insurrection.
Tomonaga (1144-1160). Killed by his father after the Heiji insurrection
rather than be left behind.
Yoritomo -- nuff said.
Yoshikado (?) Died young (early teens?) after appointment as lieutenant of
the Left Palace Guard.
Mareyoshi (?-1182) As yet a noncombatant, he was murdered in Tosa by Heike
loyalists.
Noriyori (1156-1193). Executed on Yoritomo's orders.
Zenjou (ne Imamaru, 1160-after 1180) Took Buddhist orders, supported
Yoritomo during the Genpei War.
Gien (ne Yoshimaru, 1160-1187).
Yoshitsune -- nuff said.

by fifthchamber@aol.com

I believe that Minamoto Yoshitsune was the youngest of the three named here,
although the Minamoto clan was not only confined to the Seiwa branch of the
clan that produced the three here...Yoshitsune would have been a half brother
to Yoritomo because of the system of marriage in Heian Japan, that would have
meant that Yoshitsune was born to a consort of Minamoto Yoshitomo. However
there were, I believe, four sons born to Yoshitomo and one Daughter, Gien.
The sons names were Yoshihira, Yoritomo, Noriyori, and Yoshitsune (Who was
the youngest). Yoshihira lived between 1141-1160 (Full name; Kamakura no
Akugenda.) He was captured and killed after the Heiji disturbance of 1159.
Sorry not to help out more but there are plenty of books to look at in
research, the best being Heike Monogatari (The tale of the
Heike-H.C.McCullough, Stanford uni. press.). good luck on the project!!
Ben Sharples.

Dear Shishi,

I have consulted E. Papinot's "Historical and Geographical Dictionary of Japan"
on your question (it is all I have!!) and found the following:

> Ise no Saburo
No reference.

> the Sato brothers
page 547: "Sato: Daimyo family, descended from Fujiwara Hidesato, possesing the
fief of Shinobu (Mutsu) since the 11th century. Besides Saigyo-Hoshi, the best
known members of the family are the two brothers Tsuginobu (1158-1185) and
Tadanobu (1160-1185), sons of Motoharu. They both belonged to the 4 bodyguards
(shi-tenno) of Miyamoto Yoshitsune, and died in his cause."

> the Kamada brothers???
No reference.
