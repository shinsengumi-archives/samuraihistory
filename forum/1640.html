Just to let you know that the que resting on the shaved part of the
head was one of several hairstyles worn by the bushi. It has come
down to us as the most seen in movies. If you look at old prints
though a number of styles are shown. The on that I personally have
seen the most in my limited experience ,other than the que on top, is
where the que basicaly sticks straight up in back and the end is
fanned out in a half circle. There were however other styles that
were worn.

Ever wonder why a Japanese bandit covers his hair?
One reason is the dust, under floorboards and under eaves, it
would stick in the heavily oiled hair and it would be obvious to
anyone that you had been crawling around above the ceiling. More
importantly though is the hairstyle. The Nihon Rekishi Zuroku shows
some 60 hairstyles for men and over 100 for women. Each is for a
particular caste or occupation. Edo residents would instantly
recognize a persons status and even occupation by their hairstyle.
For them it would be easy to see and remember even in the dark.
By covering your hair you could enter and a house and even if
seen slip back into the crowd just by uncovering your head!
