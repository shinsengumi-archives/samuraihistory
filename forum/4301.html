Takki to Play Yoshitsune

by kitsuno

From 'Japan Zone'

Takizawa Hideaki (22), formerly of pop group Johnny's Junior and now one half of
the duo Takki and Tsubasa, has been chosen to play the lead in next year's NHK
taiga drama, "Yoshitsune". It tells the story of the warlord Minamotono Yoshitsune
(1159-89), a major figure at the end of the Heian Period. A total of 34 veteran
actors and newcomers have been named in the cast. Among the popular female stars
making their taiga debuts will be Ueto Aya and and former Morning Musume Goto Maki
(both 18). Others include Koizumi Kotaro, son of the prime minister, comedian
Nanbara Kiyotaka of Un-chan Nan-chan, and veteran Tanba Tetsuro.

Since no one else has answered this, I will attempt.
The Yoshitsune Taiga drama will be about the life of
Minamoto Yoshitsune, the younger brother of Minamoto
Yoritomo, the first Minamoto Shogun. Yoshitsune is a
historical figure, though much legend clouds fact. He
was a Minamoto general and primary field commander,
and after subjugating his unruly cousin Yoshinaka, he
succeeded in leading the Minamoto to victory against
the Taira clan. Once this was done, however, Yoritomo
took steps to eliminate all rivals for political
power, and this included Yoshitsune. Yoshitsune was
hunted down and killed, his wife, some followers, and
his sidekick Benkei at his side.

I don't know the manga to which you refer, but unless
it's about this Yoshitsune, I doubt it's the basis for
the drama. Past Taiga drama have used literary sources
for inspiration (recent example being 1996's
"Hideyoshi" and 2003's "Miyamoto Musashi" drawing
heavily from Yoshikawa Eiji's work, though I'd
hesitate to say they were COMPLETELY based on his
books).

Though with recent trends in Taiga drama, maybe Manga
is a possible source of their inspiration--they've
tried everything else to increase ratings.
