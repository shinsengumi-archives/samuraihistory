A Question on Samurai Military Evolution

by amajinnokaze

Hajimemashite, mina. Boku wa JC desu. Dozo Yoroshiku. Now that we
have introductions out of the way, I wished to ask about the evolution of
the individualistc 900-1100s samurai using horse bows to the conception
of the primarily katana wielding samurai that you see in most depictions of
the samurai. I know that the Mongolian invasion was when samurai battle
tactics really started to change to more unit-oriented style, but I am
unfamilar with when the transition to swords from bows occured and the
how and why?

RE: [samuraihistory] A Question on Samurai Military Evolution

by Michael Peters

JC,

Your timeline is reasonably accurate, however it's bows, spears and THEN
(after peace broke out) swords. In modern terms the sword was more the ankle
holstered backup than anything else.That's not to say they weren't
important, they were also the "bling". Daimyo wrote to thier descendents and
retainers not to spend huge amounts on famous swords but to instead spend
those funds training spearmen.

Re: A Question on Samurai Military Evolution

by drnostrand

Hi.

A lot of battle paintings of the sengoku period show samurai units using weapons other
than swords as their primary weapon. I think that you are thinking more of the Edo period
where male members of the buke class customarily wore two swords when out doors, but
did not generally carry other weapons.
