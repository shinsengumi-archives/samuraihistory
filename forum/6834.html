Re Tokugawa Ieyasu's uma jirushi

by deanwayland

Hi Kitsuno-dono,

A minor correction, re Tokugawa Ieyasu's uma jirushi shown as No.4 at:

http://www.samurai-archives.com/mon5.html

Having had the good fortune to be able to visit the "Shogun" exhibition
at the Royal Armouries in Leeds, where they had the Sekigahara screen
and Ieyasu's uma jirushi on display, two things:

1. On the screen it "is" shown as per the above illustration.
2. However, the actual object does not have the red sun, it is quite
plain gold leaf. There was a note to the effect that it has often been
incorrectly illustrated with the sun on it.

BTW the dam thing is huge! We measured the length of the limbs of the
fan, and they came out to circa 7 feet! And IIRC a caption stated that
it was carried attached to the side of a pole 5.35m (17.5 feet) tall!
Which having recently carried a simple linen uma jirushi (5 x 6 feet) on
a 15 foot pole in a display, is no mean feat.

Yours

Dean
