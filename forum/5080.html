Someone may have already mentioned it, but Varley's
"The Onin War" is THE source in English. I reread it a
few months back.

For armor, etc., try Stephen Turnbull's "Samurai
Warriors". For all of Turnbull's faults, that's a real
good one as far as showing the particular armor of
each time period, and how it adapted and evolved.
Great color prints.

Also for weapons/armor I'd try Bottomley and Hopson's
"Arms and Armour of the Samurai", as that does a good
job of showing time periods also.

For tactics, there isn't a whole lot out there for
this time period--mainly because there wasn't a whole
lot documented about the actual tactics of the time
period. Of course, most of the fighting at the
beginning of the Onin War as house to house street
fighting in the capital, which doesn't take much
tactical genius--circle a house, light it on fire, and
kill the enemy as he comes out seems to be a common
version.
