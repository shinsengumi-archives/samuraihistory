by geregjonesmuller

Gentles:
I receive this list in digest form, so before I answer anybody's notes
directly I'd just like to note that I'm delighted at where this thread is
starting to go. Whatever I feel from my blades is only one person's
perception - and a modern person's at that, however deeply immersed (some
would say submerged) I might be in ancient traditions. Whether anybody
believes me or not can make no difference to my perception, of course; and
likewise, my individual - and entirely subjective - perception is highly
unlikely to sway anyone else's belief. And that's fine too.
What I'm seeing here is, I think, of more importance to the group. I
didn't intend to start - or to contribute to - an argument. I did hope to
see this dialogue come up: informed discussion of the idea in the context
of Japanese (and, by way of perspective, other cultures') beliefs in the
matter.
I'm learning here, gentlemen; and I thank you.
Gereg

P.S. For you other 'digest' recipients: _please_, when you're answering a
post, delete the other irrelevant posts. I've been getting a string of five
or six consecutive messages unrelated to current topics for the better part
of a week. I mean, sure, those are some good messages: but.
G.

wrote:

Whatever I feel from my blades is
> only one person's

A key point for us all to keep in mind. Subjective
threads like this one arouse more emotion than they
should, especially since there isn't a "right" or
"wrong". Now, if you want to start quoting Stephen
Hayes and tell me that Nobunaga's son Katsuyori
attacked a ninja clan because his dad fell off a
horse...

(Explanation: I'm currently putting myself through the
agony of reading his book--and I use that term only
because it has pages and is bound--about the mystical
origins of Ninjutsu--insert appropriate oohs and ahs
here. The endstate is a review on Amazon so that no
one every BUYS this filth--I borrowed it from my
library. Anyways, none of Nobunaga's sons, as most of
you know, were named Katsuyori. Takeda Katsuyori was
the enemy he defeated at Nagashino. Anyone doing
"research" for a "book" might want to at least know
THAT much history, but I digress.)

Good point, and a reminder on some posting etiquette:
Please, if you can, delete whatever in the message you
are replying to that doesn't actually have a bearing
on your reply. This includes those annoying little
signatures at the bottom of emails, the Yahoo ads,
etc. And for most, it's helpful if you either
in-context post, or bottom post. If you post at the
top of a reply, one has to search through the message
at the bottom to see the message to which you are
replying, and that can get confusing.

Thanks,
